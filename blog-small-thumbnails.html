Forearm model for working out intradermal injections

A simulator for practicing intravenous injection skills.

The simulator is a model of an adult hand from the forearm to the hand with valves and an imitation of a short sleeve of clothing, designed to practice the skills of injections and venipuncture, the introduction of peripheral intravenous catheters. The model is covered with a material that imitates the texture of human skin, and has an anatomically accurate reproduction of the texture of the muscles of the hand. Replacement inserts for intramuscular injections are easily replaced. The simulator allows you to practice venipuncture skills with a realistic feeling of needle failure during the procedure and administration of medicines.

A simulator for practicing intravenous injection skills.

The hand simulator is designed to teach the technique of infusion-transfusion therapy (including in extreme situations), intravenous injections and blood collection. The simulator-arm for dummies can be used for training students of medical institutes, students of medical academies, doctors of non-surgical profile, students of medical institutions. The simulator is a model of a person's arm (shoulder, forearm and hand) mounted on a stand. The area of the elbow bend is removable. In the elbow bend of the arm, 2 veins protrude, imitated by a rubber tube. After repeated injections, holes may form in the rubber tubes, through which the liquid will seep. In these cases, the handset should be changed. If the "skin" of the elbow joint is penetrated , the entire removable part of the simulator should be replaced.

Intramuscular injection simulator with indicator.

Medical Training Simulator for enemas and intramuscular injections» The simulator can be used to teach students of secondary and higher medical schools the skills of enema procedures and intramuscular injections. The simulator is a truncated lower part of the human torso with a soft insert for intramuscular injections and an anal opening for enemas. An enema is a procedure for injecting fluid into the lower segment of the rectum for therapeutic or diagnostic purposes, performed using a catheter, an Esmarch cup, or a rubber enema with a plastic tip. The procedure is usually performed in a lying position on the left side with the legs bent, pulled up to the stomach. The simulator has overall dimensions of 16 x 70 x 20 cm.
